# Privacy & Infrastructure

Enhance your digital privacy and infrastructure using Bitcoin-friendly services.

## VPN Services

### What is a VPN?

A Virtual Private Network (VPN) encrypts your internet connection and hides your IP address, providing:
- Online anonymity and privacy
- Bypassing geographic restrictions
- Protection on public Wi-Fi
- Encrypted internet traffic
- Protection from surveillance

### Choosing a VPN

### Key Considerations

#### Privacy Jurisdiction
- Countries with strong privacy laws
- Avoid Five Eyes, Nine Eyes, Fourteen Eyes
- No mandatory data retention laws
- Independent legal systems

#### No-Logs Policy
- Verified no-logs (audited)
- Strong privacy commitment
- Data retention policies
- Transparency reports
- Independent audits

#### Features
- Kill switch
- DNS leak protection
- Split tunneling
- Multi-hop
- Obfuscation
- WireGuard protocol

#### Performance
- Connection speeds
- Server locations
- Bandwidth limits
- Simultaneous connections
- Streaming capability

### Payment Methods
- Many VPNs accept Bitcoin directly
- Some accept other cryptocurrencies
- Some require account (email)
- Mullvad accepts Bitcoin without account
- Some offer payment via third-party

### Privacy Best Practices

#### VPN Security
- Enable kill switch
- Use DNS leak protection
- Check for WebRTC leaks
- Use WireGuard if available
- Regularly test leaks

#### Account Privacy
- Use burner email if account required
- Consider privacy-focused email (ProtonMail)
- Don't use personal information
- Consider prepaid payment methods
- Use strong, unique password

#### Operational Security
- Connect before sensitive browsing
- Disconnect when not needed
- Use VPN for all traffic
- Consider browser extensions
- Verify IP address changes

### VPN Setup Tips
- Follow provider's setup guides
- Test connection immediately
- Verify no DNS leaks
- Configure kill switch
- Enable auto-connect if desired
- Save configuration

## Summary

Privacy and infrastructure are foundational for digital sovereignty. Use Bitcoin-friendly VPNs, email providers, and hosting services to enhance your privacy. Consider self-hosting for maximum control. Balance security, privacy, and usability.

**Best Practice**: Layer your privacy tools. Use VPN, encrypted email, and privacy-focused services together. Understand that privacy is not anonymity, and choose tools based on your threat model.
