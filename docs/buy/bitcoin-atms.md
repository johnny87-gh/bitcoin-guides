# Bitcoin ATMs

Bitcoin ATMs (BTMs) provide a convenient way to purchase Bitcoin with cash, often without identity verification for smaller amounts.

## What are Bitcoin ATMs?

Bitcoin ATMs are physical kiosks that allow you to:
- Buy Bitcoin with cash
- Sell Bitcoin for cash (some machines)
- Instant transaction processing
- Simple user experience

## Types of Bitcoin ATMs

### One-Way ATMs
- Only allow purchasing Bitcoin
- More common in many locations
- Simpler interface
- Lower operational costs
- Limited functionality

### Two-Way ATMs
- Allow buying AND selling Bitcoin
- More complex and costly to operate
- May have lower limits
- Additional verification often required
- More flexible use

## How to Use a Bitcoin ATM

### Buying Bitcoin

#### Step 1: Find a Bitcoin ATM
- Use a locator service or app
- Check machine status and operating hours
- Verify supported cryptocurrencies
- Check current fees and rates

#### Step 2: Prepare Your Wallet
- Have a Bitcoin wallet ready
- Generate a receiving address
- Test wallet functionality first
- Understand Bitcoin network basics

#### Step 3: Visit the ATM
- Ensure you have enough cash
- Bring your smartphone with wallet
- Have your Bitcoin address accessible
- Allow time for the process

#### Step 4: Start Transaction
- Select "Buy Bitcoin" option
- Enter or scan your Bitcoin address
- Insert cash into machine
- Verify transaction details

#### Step 5: Confirm and Receive
- Verify all details carefully
- Confirm the transaction
- Wait for Bitcoin to arrive
- Check your wallet for confirmation

### Selling Bitcoin (Two-Way ATMs)

#### Step 1: Find Selling ATM
- Search specifically for two-way ATMs
- Verify machine supports selling
- Check current buy-back rate
- Verify withdrawal limits

#### Step 2: Prepare Transaction
- Have Bitcoin in mobile wallet
- Generate receiving address for Bitcoin sale
- Check network fees
- Ensure you have enough Bitcoin

#### Step 3: Visit ATM
- Allow sufficient time
- Have smartphone ready
- Understand the process beforehand
- Be prepared for ID verification

#### Step 4: Complete Sale
- Select "Sell Bitcoin" option
- Send Bitcoin to provided address
- Wait for confirmations
- Collect cash dispensed

## Fees and Limits

### Fee Structure
- **Average fees**: 7-20% per transaction
- **Fixed fees**: Often $5-15 per transaction
- **Percentage fees**: Additional 5-15% of transaction
- **Network fees**: Added to Bitcoin transaction fee

### Fee Examples
- $100 purchase might cost $110-120 total
- $500 purchase might cost $530-560 total
- $1,000 purchase might cost $1,070-1,150 total

### Transaction Limits

#### Without ID Verification
- **Typical range**: $500-$5,000
- **Common default**: $1,000-$3,000
- **Time restrictions**: Daily and monthly limits
- **Vary by operator and location**

#### With ID Verification
- **Typical range**: $5,000-$29,000
- **Often requires**: Phone number, government ID, SSN
- **Additional verification**: Facial recognition possible
- **Longer setup time**: 5-10 minutes for verification

### Factors Affecting Limits
- Operator policies
- Local regulations
- Machine capabilities
- User verification level
- Payment method

## Pros and Cons

### Advantages
- **Privacy**: No KYC for small amounts
- **Convenience**: Simple, quick transactions
- **Cash payments**: Use physical cash
- **Accessibility**: Available in various locations
- **Instant**: Immediate Bitcoin transfer
- **No bank required**: Good for unbanked users

### Disadvantages
- **High fees**: Most expensive way to buy Bitcoin
- **Lower limits**: Daily and monthly restrictions
- **Limited locations**: Not available everywhere
- **Poor rates**: Above-market pricing
- **Potential scams**: Fake ATMs exist (rare)
- **Technical issues**: Machines can malfunction

## Security Considerations

### Physical Security
- Use ATMs in secure, well-lit locations
- Be aware of your surroundings
- Avoid using ATMs late at night
- Consider bringing a friend
- Trust your instincts

### Transaction Security
- Verify Bitcoin address before sending
- Use trusted wallet apps
- Double-check transaction amounts
- Keep transaction receipts
- Monitor your wallet

### Privacy Protection
- Understand that machine may record you
- Be aware of camera surveillance
- Don't share personal information unnecessarily
- Use privacy-focused wallets
- Consider using mixing services for privacy

## ID Verification Process

### Why Verification Required
- Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance
- Know Your Customer (KYC) regulations
- Operator risk management
- Legal requirements by jurisdiction
- Larger transaction amounts

### Common Verification Requirements
- **Phone number**: SMS verification
- **Government ID**: Driver's license, passport
- **SSN**: For US machines
- **Selfie**: Facial recognition
- **Fingerprint**: Some advanced machines

### Verification Process
- Typically takes 5-10 minutes
- Done at the machine or via phone
- Information stored by operator
- Used for future transactions
- May be shared with authorities

## Common Issues and Solutions

### Machine Malfunctions
- Try a different machine
- Contact operator support
- Check machine status online
- Report issues to operator

### Transaction Delays
- Bitcoin network congestion
- Waiting for confirmations
- Check transaction on blockchain explorer
- Contact support if issues persist

### Address Errors
- Always double-check address
- Use QR code scanning when possible
- Test address with small amount first
- Contact support immediately if wrong address

### Payment Issues
- Ensure cash is accepted by machine
- Check bill condition (torn, marked)
- Use smaller denominations if needed
- Have alternative payment ready

### High Fees
- Compare different ATMs
- Consider alternative buying methods
- Larger transactions may have lower percentage fees
- Check operator-specific promotions

## Tips for Using Bitcoin ATMs

### Before Using
- Find multiple nearby ATMs
- Compare fees and limits
- Check machine status
- Read user reviews
- Have backup options

### During Transaction
- Take your time, don't rush
- Verify all information carefully
- Keep transaction receipt
- Check your wallet for confirmation
- Be aware of your surroundings

### After Transaction
- Secure your Bitcoin properly
- Transfer to secure wallet if needed
- Record transaction for tax purposes
- Report any issues to operator
- Rate machine if possible

## Summary

Bitcoin ATMs offer convenience and privacy for buying Bitcoin with cash, but come with higher fees and lower limits. Use them for convenience, but consider other methods for larger purchases or regular buying.

**Best for**: First-time Bitcoin buyers, privacy-conscious users, unbanked individuals, and those preferring cash transactions.
