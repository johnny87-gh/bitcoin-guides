# Regulatory & Compliance Information

Navigate the legal and regulatory landscape surrounding Bitcoin.

## Understanding Bitcoin Regulation

Bitcoin's decentralized nature presents unique regulatory challenges. Different countries have taken various approaches:

- **Permissive**: Bitcoin is legal with minimal restrictions
- **Restricted**: Bitcoin is legal but subject to regulation
- **Prohibited**: Bitcoin is banned or heavily restricted

## Global Regulatory Approaches

### United States
- **SEC**: Treats Bitcoin as a commodity, not a security
- **IRS**: Classifies Bitcoin as property for tax purposes
- **CFTC**: Oversees Bitcoin futures and derivatives
- **FinCEN**: Regulates Bitcoin businesses as money service businesses

### European Union
- AMLD5 Directive: Requires KYC/AML for cryptocurrency exchanges
- MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets): Comprehensive regulatory framework
- Individual countries may have additional regulations

### Asia-Pacific
- **Japan**: Legal payment method, exchanges licensed
- **Singapore**: Progressive regulation, clear guidelines
- **Australia**: Legal with tax implications
- **China**: Cryptocurrency exchanges and mining banned

### Other Notable Jurisdictions
- **El Salvador**: Bitcoin is legal tender
- **Switzerland**: Progressive crypto-friendly regulations
- **Portugal**: Tax-friendly for cryptocurrency trading
- **Malta**: Previously crypto-friendly, now tightening regulations

| Canada | Capital gains | 50% taxable |
| Japan | Other income | Up to 55% tax rate |

### US Tax Specifics

**Short-term vs Long-term Capital Gains**
- Held <1 year: Ordinary income tax rates (10-37%)
- Held >1 year: Long-term capital gains rates (0, 15, or 20%)

**Taxable Events**
- Selling Bitcoin for fiat currency
- Trading Bitcoin for other cryptocurrencies
- Using Bitcoin for purchases
- Mining Bitcoin (treated as income)

**Non-Taxable Events**
- Buying Bitcoin with fiat currency
- Transferring Bitcoin between personal wallets
- Gifting Bitcoin (subject to gift tax limits)

### Record-Keeping Requirements

Essential records for tax purposes:
- Date of acquisition
- Cost basis (purchase price in fiat)
- Date of sale or exchange
- Fair market value at time of transaction
- Transaction fees paid
- All wallet addresses used

## Anti-Money Laundering (AML) & Know Your Customer (KYC)

### What is KYC/AML?

- **KYC (Know Your Customer)**: Identity verification processes
- **AML (Anti-Money Laundering)**: Procedures to prevent illegal financial activities

### KYC Requirements

Common KYC requirements on exchanges:
- Government-issued ID verification
- Proof of address (utility bills, bank statements)
- Selfie verification
- Source of funds declaration
- Enhanced due diligence for large transactions

### AML Compliance

- Transaction monitoring for suspicious activity
- Reporting large or unusual transactions
- Sanctions screening
- Customer due diligence

## Exchange Regulations

### Licensed vs Unlicensed Exchanges

**Licensed Exchanges**
- Subject to regulatory oversight
- Must implement KYC/AML procedures
- Required to maintain proper reserves
- Provide consumer protections

**Unlicensed Exchanges**
- May operate in regulatory gray areas
- Typically less stringent on KYC
- Higher risk for users
- May face regulatory action

### Choosing Compliant Exchanges

Factors to consider:
- Regulatory licensing
- Security measures and insurance
- Proof of reserves
- Transparency and audits
- Jurisdiction of operation

## Travel Rule

The Travel Rule requires financial institutions to pass certain information about the originator and beneficiary of cryptocurrency transactions.

- Applied in many jurisdictions since 2021
- Affects cryptocurrency exchanges and financial institutions
- Reduces anonymity in transactions between regulated entities
- Thresholds vary by jurisdiction

## Reporting Requirements

### Large Transaction Reporting

- Thresholds vary by jurisdiction
- Typically $10,000 USD equivalent or more
- Must be reported to financial authorities
- Applies to exchanges and other regulated entities

### Suspicious Activity Reporting

- Exchanges must report unusual transaction patterns
- Potential money laundering or terrorist financing
- Anonymous or encrypted transactions
- Rapid movement of funds across jurisdictions

## Banking Relationships

### Challenges for Bitcoin Businesses

- Difficulty obtaining traditional bank accounts
- De-banking risks
- Increased compliance costs
- Limited payment processing options

### Bank Responses

- Some banks have embraced Bitcoin services
- Others have restricted or banned Bitcoin-related transactions
- Varying policies by jurisdiction

## Self-Custody vs Custodial Services

### Self-Custody
- No regulatory reporting for personal use
- Responsibility for security falls on user
- Greater privacy but requires more knowledge

### Custodial Services
- Subject to regulatory oversight
- Must comply with KYC/AML
- Often insured but less private

## Securities Regulation

### Bitcoin's Status

- Most regulators classify Bitcoin as a commodity
- Not considered a security by the SEC
- Some tokens may be considered securities

### Implications

- Different regulatory treatment than securities
- Different tax treatment
- Fewer disclosure requirements
- Different investor protections

## Legal Considerations for Individuals

### Staying Compliant

- Maintain accurate records
- Report taxable events
- Use regulated exchanges for significant transactions
- Understand local laws and regulations
- Consult tax professionals

### Protecting Your Rights

- Understand legal status in your jurisdiction
- Keep detailed records
- Be aware of reporting requirements
- Use compliant services when possible

## Tips for Staying Compliant

1. **Educate Yourself**: Understand the regulations in your jurisdiction
2. **Keep Records**: Document all transactions meticulously
3. **Use Licensed Exchanges**: Prefer regulated service providers
4. **Consult Professionals**: Speak with tax advisors or lawyers
5. **Stay Updated**: Regulations are constantly evolving
6. **Be Transparent**: Avoid trying to hide activities from authorities

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Not reporting taxable events
- Assuming Bitcoin is unregulated
- Using only unregulated services
- Failing to keep proper records
- Not understanding local laws
- Ignoring AML/KYC requirements
